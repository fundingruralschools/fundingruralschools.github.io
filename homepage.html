What is the Secure Rural Schools Program?

Following the creation of the United States Forest Service in 1905, the Act of May 23, 1908--today, known as the Secure Rural Schools and Community Self Determination Act. This act was meant to help compensate counties with growing amounts of forest service land for the potential tax money which they were losing by not building on the acres of protected land.Payments to counties are broken into three categories: Title I for roads and schools, Title II for projects on federal lands, and Title III for county projects.


To learn more about the origins of the Secure Rural Schools Program, click here to visit the National Forest Service website.
